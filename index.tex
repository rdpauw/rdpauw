\subsection{Profile}\label{profile}

Public-health researcher combining expertise in health sciences with
advanced methodological skills in epidemiology, statistics and data
science. My work bridges the individual (clinical) and population
(epidemiological) levels, using linked patient and administrative data
to identify gaps in care and applying advanced analytics to support
causal, evidence-based decision-making. Core interests include
burden-of-disease modelling, health inequalities, causal inference,
biostatistics, and the translation of research into health policy.
Experienced in leading multidisciplinary projects, securing competitive
funding, and supervising students and doctoral researchers, with
sustained work in both R and SAS.

\subsection{Selected research achievements}\label{achievements}

\textbf{NEED project (Needs Examination, Evaluation and Dissemination).}
Developed a methodological framework to systematically map unmet
health-related needs, validated at EU level through a modified Delphi
study. Secured BELSPO funding, designed the conceptual framework, and
built the project dashboard and dataflow.

\textbf{High-impact fundamental research in \emph{PAIN®}.} First-author
graph-theoretical brain-network analysis in chronic neck pain,
recognised as runner-up for best oral presentation at the European
Congress of Manual Therapy.

\textbf{Large-scale population cohorts and data-linkage.} Designed
complex population-level linkage projects including HELICON (COVID-19
health inequalities), the TeleHealth study, and a national long-COVID
follow-up study.

\textbf{Recognised (inter)national analytical expertise.} Invited as an
analytical expert on projects including a Scottish excess-weight
modelling study (2003--2040), fungal diversity in bats, and validation
of the SPIDER hypermobility questionnaire.
